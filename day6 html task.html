FORTUNER

Launched in 2004 as part of Toyota’s IMV project and built on the rugged Hilux pickup platform, the Toyota Fortuner has evolved from a utilitarian SUV into a premium, tech-loaded, seven-seater, becoming a dominant force in the segment. It entered India in 2009 with a 3.0L diesel, later adding petrol, automatic, and 4x4 variants.

The Hindustan Contessa, produced by Hindustan Motors from 1984 to 2002, was a luxury sedan often considered India's first "muscle car" due to its long-hood, rear-wheel-drive design, inspired by the British Vauxhall Victor FE. It was favored by government officials and, in later years, featured powerful 1.8L Isuzu petrol engines.

Origin: Based on the Vauxhall VX Series (1976–1978), which was a development of the Vauxhall Victor FE. Production: Manufactured in India by Hindustan Motors from 1984–2002. Engines: Early models used a 1.5L engine (50bhp), but it became famous for the 1.8L Isuzu petrol and 2.0L Isuzu diesel engines. Legacy: Known for its spacious, comfortable, and classic design, often used in Indian cinema as a "villain's car". Late 1980s-90s: Upgraded to more powerful 1.8L Isuzu petrol and diesel engines. 2002: Discontinued due to stricter emission norms and declining demand
